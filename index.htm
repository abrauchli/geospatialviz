Visualization of TODO Data

The page is divided into four tabs: the visualization (map), the raw data, the help section and the sources.

If you're running this page directly from your local filesystem and the map remains grey and the data on the second tab is empty, see the setup instructions for help.

﻿

This map works on the premise of mapping multiple inputs. The user may select any number of states, but only one country followed by a combination of the years 2009 to 2012. The selection will then be tallied and passed into the charts located at the bottom of the screen. In the case where the user wishes to select all the states to tally up the country's gross import or export, simply deselect all of the states and by default the input will be the sum of all states. Or simply refresh the page, the default selection is no states. On initial load, all three charts, histogram, pie chart, and column chart; are displayed. The user may remove/add the charts by clicking “Menu” which expands into a choice of pie chart, column chart and histogram, which will remove or add them depending on whether or not they’re currently being displayed. While viewing the charts the user may feel as though it is difficult to view the data of a chart. You may expand a chart by clicking on the chart element and pulling it up on the bottom left window, which will allow the user to zoom onto the the element. Once the element appears in the zoom window click on the zoom window to enlarge the chart. The default page is “Commodities by U.S. state” to change your view choose from the tabs at the top, specifically if you wish to see trade on the international scale select, “International Imports/Exports.” However unlike in the state selections, here you select the states on the selection window as before and the country they are based on the larger window on the right. Finally, the “Raw Data” tab features a table where its rows are broken down by state in relation to either commodity or country. Each column contains a quantity showing the breakdown between the state and country/commodity such as quantity per year, % share and changes in percent.
